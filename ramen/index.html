The History of Ramen

Ramen originated in China like 400-500 years ago before making its way to Japan and was used in Japanese cuisine. The men in ramen is 麺, "noodle", which is the same character as the Chinese 麵 employed in chow mein.

At the beginning people only eat noodle without any sauce. About 200 years later, some people put soy sauce in it, and that is the first Ramen. By 1900, restaurants serving Chinese cuisine from Canton and Shanghai offered a simple ramen dish of noodles (cut rather than hand-pulled), a few toppings, and a broth flavored with salt and pork bones.

Ramen noodles are made from wheat flour and kansui which containing sodium carbonate. Ane the soup is mostly made from stock based on chicken, pork or beef bones, and flavoured with salt, miso or soy sauce.
